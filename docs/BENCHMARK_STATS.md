## Key Statistics

### Diagnostic Delay Summary
- **Longest delay:** Podoconiosis — decade or more (patients symptomatic from childhood, diagnosed in third decade)
- **Shortest delay:** Rhodesiense HAT — 5 days misdiagnosed, but fatal within weeks without treatment
- **Most catastrophic delay:** Female Genital Schistosomiasis — 56 million women affected across sub-Saharan Africa, only ~15,000 ever formally assessed
- **Average documented delay across 15 cases:** ~3–5 years
- **Cases where delay contributed to irreversible damage:** 9/15 (60%)

### Why These Cases Are Diagnostically Hard
- **Anchoring bias:** 12/15 cases misdiagnosed as the highest-prevalence condition in that region
- **Missing tests:** 10/15 required tests not routinely available in the country of presentation
- **Symptom overlap:** All 15 cases share ≥3 symptoms with the wrong diagnosis
- **Geographic blindspot:** 8/15 diseases considered "not present" in that African sub-region by local clinicians
- **Time to seek care:** Average estimated 6–18 months before first medical contact in rural cases

## Why This Matters for MedSyn Investigator

The combined diagnostic delay across these 15 cases represents an estimated **47+ patient-years** of suffering from wrong or delayed diagnosis. MedSyn Investigator is designed to surface the correct rare diagnosis at first clinical contact by:

1. Cross-referencing symptoms against Orphanet, PubMed, and WHO regional data simultaneously
2. Applying geographic epidemiological context (altitude, soil, endemic zones)
3. Flagging "treatment non-response" as a discriminator signal
4. Recognizing multi-system non-classic clusters that single-specialty clinicians miss

These 15 benchmark cases represent diseases that took real patients between **5 days and 10+ years** to diagnose correctly. The benchmark measures whether MedSyn can close that gap at scale.
